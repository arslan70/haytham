## 1. Problem Analysis (Top 3 Problems)

Identify the top 3 SPECIFIC problems this idea solves. For each problem:

**Required Format:**
```
**Problem [N]: [One-line problem statement]**
- **Trigger Moment:** "The user reaches for this product when ___"
  - Must be a specific moment: context + state of mind + action
  - BAD: "when they want to save money" (too vague)
  - GOOD: "when they open the grocery flyer email and realize matching recipes will take 30+ minutes"
- **Trigger Confidence:** [Observed | Inferred | Constructed]
  - Observed: Directly described or strongly implied by the founder's input
  - Inferred: Reasonable extrapolation from the idea, but not stated
  - Constructed: Had to imagine a scenario — founder didn't describe this pain
  - RULE: If Constructed, add a one-line note explaining WHY you had to manufacture this trigger
- **Current Workaround:** What do people do today? Why inadequate?
  - **Effort:** [Low | Medium | High] — how much work does the current workaround require?
  - Time estimates MUST include sanity check: "[estimate: X mins] — assumes [conditions]"
- **Pain Intensity:** [Exactly one of: Low | Medium | High]
  - Low: Annoying but tolerated; won't pay to solve
  - Medium: Causes friction; would try a free solution
  - High: Urgent/blocking; actively seeking and paying for solutions
  - RULE: Pick the LOWER level if between two. Not all problems are High.
```

Avoid generic problems like "market uncertainty" or "resource constraints" - these apply to ALL startups.

## 2. Target User Segments (2-3 segments)

Define segments by BEHAVIOR, not demographics.

**PROHIBITED (will invalidate your output):**
- Age ranges ("ages 25-45")
- Gender, income brackets, geographic location
- Lifestyle labels without behavior ("busy professionals", "eco-conscious families")

**REQUIRED - Behavioral definition:**
- What they DO: actions, habits, tools they use
- Where they gather: specific forums, communities, apps
- What they've tried: past solutions attempted

**Format per segment:**
```
**[Primary/Secondary] Segment: [Behavioral description]**
- **Defining Behavior:** [Specific action they take regularly]
- **Where to Find Them:** [Specific online/offline location]
- **Trigger Context:** [When/where they experience the problem]
- **Budget Indicator:** [student / professional discretionary / enterprise] [needs validation]
- **Urgency Driver:** [Why now, not later? What forces action?]
```

**Example:**
- BAD: "Busy professionals ages 25-45 who want to save money"
- GOOD: "People who open grocery store apps weekly to check sales but don't follow through on meal planning"

## 3. Unique Value Proposition (UVP)

**Format:** "[Target user] can [ONE specific, measurable outcome]."

**Measurability Test - your UVP must pass ONE of these:**
- Contains a number: "in under 5 minutes", "save $50/week", "3x faster"
- Contains a concrete deliverable: "get a weekly meal plan", "receive matched recipes"
- Describes elimination of a specific pain: "without checking multiple apps"

**Validation:**
- [ ] ONE outcome only (no "and")
- [ ] Fits in a tweet (< 140 chars)
- [ ] Can attach a metric to it

**Examples:**
- BAD: "Users can discover recipes perfectly matched to sales" (what does "discover" mean?)
- BAD: "Save time AND money AND reduce waste" (three outcomes)
- GOOD: "Home cooks can get a weekly meal plan matched to local sales in under 2 minutes"

## 4. Solution Concept (High-Level)

Describe WHAT the solution delivers, not HOW it's built.

**Capability vs Feature Test:**
- FEATURE: "Push notification reminders" (implementation detail)
- CAPABILITY: "Proactive time-sensitive guidance" (user outcome)
- Ask: "What job does this do for the user?" not "What does the app do?"

**Format:**
```
- **Core Value Delivery:** [The "aha moment" - what does user GET?]
- **Key Capabilities (3-5):**
  - [Capability 1] → addresses Problem [N]
  - [Capability 2] → addresses Problem [N]
  - [Capability 3] → addresses Problem [N]
```

## 6. Concept Health Signals

Assess honestly — these feed directly into downstream risk analysis. An honest "Weak" is more valuable than a fabricated "Strong."

```
- **Pain Clarity:** [Clear | Ambiguous | Weak]
  Is there a specific pain users actively try to solve today?
  If Ambiguous/Weak: [one-line explanation of what's missing]
- **Trigger Strength:** [Strong | Moderate | Weak]
  Would users reach for this at a specific, recurring moment?
  If Weak: [note whether idea is aspiration/habit-driven rather than pain-driven]
- **Willingness to Pay Signal:** [Present | Unclear | Absent]
  Is there evidence users would pay, or is this a nice-to-have?
```

CRITICAL: Do NOT soften these assessments. If most triggers above are Inferred or Constructed, Trigger Strength should be Weak — not Moderate. Consistent honesty here prevents wasted effort downstream.
